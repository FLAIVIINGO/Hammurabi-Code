Bitwise Operations

Andrew Babilonia

In this blog post, I got this idea from working through some of the practice problems in Computer Systems by Randal Bryant and David R. O'Hallaron. In chapter 2 of the book, they specifically go over bitwise and logical operations. I found one of the practice problems to be very interesting. Problem number 61 asks you to write a C expression that evaluates to 1 when true, and 0 when false. But there is a small caveat, you may not use any equality or inequality tests, loops, switch statements, function calls or macros. Division isn't allowed, modulus and multiplication. Comparisons are also not allowed. So, what are we left with? Well, we have the biwise operators, the tilde, ampersand, pipe symbol, and we also have the not logical operator. The first question asks us to evaluate an integer and return a 1 if it contains any ones in its binary form.
